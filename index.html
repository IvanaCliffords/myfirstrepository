How To Create Your First Repository

Hello! I'm Ivana!

Austin Texas

I'm going to show you how to create your first GitHub repository!

This is the start of a very exciting journey that you'll have as a software developer and I'm very happy to share this with you!

Step 1: Go to github.com and click on the "Sign up" button.

Step 2: After choosing your username / password and verifying your account, log into your GitHub account and add your profile image and a short bio.

Step 3: In order to create your first repository, click on a “+” symbol on the upper right corner of your screen and select “New repository”.

Step 4: Give your repository a name, check a “Add a README file” button, and click “Create repository” button.

Step 5: Now that you have a repository, you need to upload the files that are acossiated with your repository.

Step 6: Drag your files into designated space.

Step 7: Add "Initial file upload" as a description and click on "Commit changes" button.

Step 8: Click on "Settings" button, scroll down to the "GitHub Pages" section, and change the source from "none" to "main" and click "Save".

Congratulations! You created your first repository!
